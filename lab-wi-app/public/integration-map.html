Integration Architecture · D365 Supply Chain ↔ Rocket Ship

Who does the work — and how the two systems talk

D365 is the system of record for planning and inventory; Rocket Ship is where the Reagent Lab actually makes product. Planning flows down to the shop floor, and actuals flow back up — a continuous hand-off that keeps demand, supply, and on-hand in lock-step.

D365 — plan & inventory (system of record) Rocket Ship — production & quality execution Integration message — data passed between them
01

Who owns what

Each system is authoritative for its own half. D365 decides what to make and holds the inventory truth; Rocket Ship governs how it's made and proven.

02

How they communicate — back & forth

The plan is pushed down to Rocket Ship; every actual is posted back up to D365 so the inventory and planning picture stays true.

D365 ▶ ROCKET SHIP Plan hands off work

Downstream — planning tells the shop floor what to make.

Item master sync Products, item type (FG / RM / PKG), unit of measure, vendor. Trigger: D365 sync — keeps Rocket Ship's catalog aligned.
Planned production order Item · quantity · requirement date, pegged to its demand. Trigger: master planning run → Rocket Ship's Planned Orders queue.
ROCKET SHIP ◀ D365 Execution reports back

Upstream — the shop floor tells the record what actually happened.

Production order started Order firmed & run begins. Message: ProdProductionOrderStart → D365.
Material consumption posted Actual raw materials & lots consumed against the formula. Drives on-hand down & keeps reorder points honest.
Finished output — report as finished Product quantity produced, with QC status. Drives on-hand up → available to ship / stock.

Net effect: D365 stays the single source of truth for demand and inventory, while Rocket Ship owns the execution and quality record — neither duplicates the other's job.

Then it loops: the labs consume the replenished stock, on-hand falls back toward the reorder point, and the next master planning run starts the cycle again.
