What this section covers

This is the planning & scheduling side of the platform — how demand becomes scheduled work. Planned orders flow in from D365; you firm them into real production orders (against each item's approved Work Instruction), then schedule them onto the calendar — by hand or with the auto-scheduler that finds an available person who can finish by the requirement date. The Production Schedule (a per-person Gantt) is the picture of who's making what, when — and when someone's out, you can cover the absence in a couple of clicks. Around it sit the supporting modules: Inventory, Cycle Count, Reagent Orders, and Quality Trends.

Your role today

Approver / Admin
Reviews and firms planned orders, schedules and re-balances work, covers absences.
Operator
Reads the Production Schedule to see what's on their calendar and when.
Firming needs an approved WI. Firming a planned order builds a production order from the item's default formula — the approved Work Instruction for that reagent. No approved WI, nothing to firm against.

Walkthrough

1

Review the planned orders

ApproverAdmin
  1. Open Planned Production Orders — these arrive from D365. Toggle the Unprocessed / Firmed tabs.
  2. Each row shows the number, item, quantity, order/delivery dates, and the D365 pegging — expand a row for the detail.
  3. Watch the urgency chip off the requirement date — Overdue, Due today, Due in Nd — that's the de-facto priority.
2

Firm a planned order

ApproverAdmin

Firming turns demand into a real production order. Two ways, depending on whether you want to schedule now:

🔨 Firm
Creates the production order and drops it into Unscheduled Orders to place later (Task 3).
✨ Firm & Schedule
Firms and auto-schedules it to an available person who can finish by the requirement date — one click, done.
  • Do one at a time from a row, or tick several and use Firm selected (N) / Firm & Schedule (N) at the top.
The result banner tells you how many firmed, how many got scheduled, and how many are waiting in Unscheduled Orders.
3

Schedule the unscheduled work

Admin

Open Unscheduled Orders — production orders that don't have a slot yet (lot, WI, assignee, duration).

✨ Auto-schedule
Auto-schedule all (or selected) assigns each order to an available person who can finish by its required date, in the next open slot.
🗓 Schedule by hand
Pick a start date & time on a row to place it yourself.
When the list clears, you'll see "All caught up" — every order has a home on the calendar.
4

Read the Production Schedule

ApproverOperator
  • Open Production Schedule — a Gantt timeline with a row per person, each order shown as a block over its scheduled window.
  • Use the toolbar to change the view: 1 / 3 / 7 / 14 / 30-day presets, Today, and prev/next to move the window.
  • Each block's colour dot shows the order's status; the block's length comes straight from the Work Instruction's scheduled time.
5

Cover an absence

ApproverAdmin

Someone called in sick? On the schedule, open Cover an absence, pick the person and the dates they're out, then choose:

↪ Reassign
Moves each of their pending orders to another available person who can still finish by its required date.
✖ Unassign
Clears the assignee & time — the orders go back to Unscheduled Orders to reschedule.
Preview first: the panel lists exactly which orders will move before you commit, so there are no surprises.

If you get stuck

What's the difference between "Firm" and "Firm & Schedule"?
Firm just creates the production order and parks it in Unscheduled Orders for you to place later. Firm & Schedule also auto-assigns it to an available person who can finish by the requirement date — use it when you're happy to let the scheduler place it.
I can't firm a planned order.
Firming builds the run from the item's default formula — its approved Work Instruction. If the item has no approved WI, there's nothing to firm against. Approve a WI for that item first.
Where do firmed orders go?
To Unscheduled Orders if you used plain Firm, or straight onto the Production Schedule if you used Firm & Schedule. Either way they're now real production orders you can open and run.
How does the auto-scheduler decide?
It works earliest-required-date first, and for each order picks an available person who can fit the run (its Work Instruction's scheduled time) into an open slot before the required date. Orders it can't fit in time are flagged as late.
Why is a run so long / short on the Gantt?
A block's length is the Work Instruction's Scheduled Time (minutes). If a run looks wrong on the calendar, adjust the scheduled time on the WI — that's what the planner blocks out.
Someone's out — fastest way to handle their work?
On the Production Schedule use Cover an absence: pick the person and dates, then Reassign (push their pending orders to others who can still hit the dates) or Unassign (send them back to Unscheduled Orders). You'll see a preview of what moves before you confirm.
