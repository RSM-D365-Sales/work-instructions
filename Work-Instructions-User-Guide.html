What a Work Instruction is

A Work Instruction (WI) is the step-by-step recipe for making one reagent product — the digital replacement for the paper/Uniflow procedure. It has a header (what you're making and how long it takes) and an ordered list of steps (weigh this, mix that, adjust pH, package, QC…). Once approved, a WI is what an operator follows to run a production batch. Every WI is versioned, and no one can approve their own — the two guardrails that keep it audit-ready.

Your role today

A
Author
Creates and edits work instructions, builds the steps, and submits them for review. Cannot approve.
✓
Approver
Reviews a submitted WI and approves it, requests changes, or rejects it — but never one they authored.
Separation of duties. You can author a WI or approve a WI — just not the same one. That's why we'll pair up: you'll submit yours, and approve someone else's.

Walkthrough

1

Open or create a Work Instruction

Author
  1. From the left sidebar, open Work Instructions. You'll see the list of existing WIs with their status.
  2. Pick your own to work with — e.g. Acidified Methanol or NSE Muscles Phosphate Buffer — by clicking it. Or start fresh with the New WI button (top right).
  3. Fill in the header fields:
Title *
What the procedure is called, e.g. "Prepare 1M Sodium Hydroxide".
Product *
Start typing to search the reagent catalog. A match shows a green linked badge with its item number.
Target Molarity
Optional — the concentration you're making (e.g. 1.0 M).
Scheduled Time
Expected minutes for one run. Drives how much time the batch blocks on the schedule.
"Not linked to an item" warning? It means the product name doesn't match a catalog record, so D365 orders won't line up. Pick an item from the dropdown to link it — or leave it if you're just exploring.
2

Walk through & edit the steps

Author

Every step has a Step Name, a Step Type (which decides what it captures), an optional description, and type-specific fields. The editor gives you these tools:

☰
Step Navigator
The pinned list on the left — click any step to jump to it. Use the panel toggle to hide/show it.
+
Insert between
Hover in the gap between two steps and click the blue + to drop a new step exactly there.
⣿
Drag to reorder
Grab a step by its grip handle (or a nav row) and drag it into a new position.
⌄
Collapse / Expand all
Buttons in the "Steps" header collapse every card to titles, or open them all back up.
  1. Read through the steps top to bottom — the navigator shows the whole flow at a glance.
  2. Edit a step: open it, change its Step Type and watch the fields change (a Weigh asks for a target weight; a Dispense asks for a volume; a Record Time just needs a label).
  3. Add a step from the Add from library panel at the bottom (appends to the end), or the + between steps (inserts mid-list).
  4. Reorder, rename, or delete anything until the flow reads the way the bench actually works.
Nothing is live yet — a WI in Draft is a safe sandbox. Edits only matter once you submit and it's approved.
3

Send it for approval

Author
  1. Save your work with Save Draft (or Create Work Instruction if it's brand new).
  2. Click Submit for Review. The status moves from Draft to Pending Review.
  3. It's now read-only for you and waiting for an approver. You can't take it further — that's the point.
Tip: If Submit for Review is greyed out, the WI is brand new — create/save it once first, then the submit button turns on.
4

Approve someone else's

Approver
  1. Open a WI that is Pending Review — ideally the one a colleague just submitted, not your own.
  2. Review the steps (the navigator helps you scan them). Add a comment in the Review & Approve panel.
  3. Choose an outcome: Approve (it becomes usable for production), Request Changes (sends it back to the author), or Reject.
Can't see the approve buttons? If you authored this WI, the system hides them — someone else has to approve it. Swap and review a partner's instead.

Step type reference

Each step type captures the right data for that action — measurements get tolerance checks, timers run, timestamps get stamped. The common ones:

Gather & measure

Gather Reagents
Pick catalog items + quantities; lot-controlled ones flag a LOT badge.
Weigh
Measure a solid mass to a target, within a tolerance % (blocks if out of range).
Dispense
The liquid version of Weigh — measure a volume (mL/L/µL) to target.
Bring to Volume
Q.S. / dilute the solution to a final volume with a named diluent.

Process

Mix
Timed mixing on a stir plate (duration + speed), with a live timer at run time.
Stir / Vortex / Invert
Manual agitation — pick the method, duration, and speed.
Transfer
Move material from one vessel to another (pour, aliquot, decant, filter…).
pH Adjust
Target pH ± tolerance with an adjusting reagent; captures the meter reading.

Temperature

Heat / Cool
Bring to a target temperature (heat adds a duration; e.g. autoclave, water bath).
Freeze / Thaw
Move to or up from frozen storage at a target temperature.
Overnight Hold
A hold or incubation that runs overnight before the next step.

Finish & deliver

Cap / Seal
Cap, screw-cap, Parafilm, or seal the container.
Package & Store
Container, label reference, and destination (e.g. "2–8 °C QC area").
Print Labels
Label template + quantity for the run.

Capture & QC

Observe & Record
A verify / check / record-the-result prompt (used for the QC verification tail).
Record Time
Operator taps to stamp a timestamp — e.g. "Start time" / "End time".
Add Attachment
Attach supporting documents (PDF, image…); can be marked required.
Production Break
A divider between parts/days of a run (e.g. "Day 1 QC").

Versioning & comparing

  • Every WI carries a version number. Once one is approved, you make edits by creating a New Version — the approved one keeps running until the new version is approved in turn.
  • When there's more than one version, Compare opens a side-by-side diff so a reviewer sees exactly what changed instead of re-reading the whole thing.
  • Copy to New Item reuses a WI as the template for a different product (e.g. start "1.0 M EDTA" from "0.5 M EDTA").

If you get stuck

Why can't I edit this Work Instruction?
Editing is only open on WIs in Draft or Rejected status that you authored (admins can always edit). If it's Pending Review or Approved, it's locked — make a New Version to change an approved one.
Why don't I see the Approve buttons?
Three reasons: you're not an Approver/Admin; the WI isn't Pending Review yet; or you authored it (you can't approve your own — separation of duties). Have a partner submit one for you to approve.
What's the LOT badge on a reagent?
It marks a lot/batch-controlled material. At production time the operator must record the actual lot number before the step can be completed — the badge is just the heads-up that it'll be required.
Weigh vs. Dispense — which do I use?
Weigh is for a solid mass (g, mg, kg); Dispense is the same idea for a liquid volume (mL, L, µL). Both enforce a target ± tolerance and block completion if the measurement is out of range.
How do I move a step, or drop one in the middle?
Drag it by the grip handle (or the row in the left navigator) to reorder, or use the up/down arrows on the card. To insert mid-list, hover in the gap between two steps and click the blue +, then pick a step type.
I made a mistake after submitting — can I take it back?
Ask an approver to Request Changes (or Reject) — that returns it to an editable state so you can fix it and resubmit. The comment they leave tells you what to change.
